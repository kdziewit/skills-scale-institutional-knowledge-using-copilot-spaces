# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA / Test Engineer

### Role Summary
QA / Test Engineers own quality validation across the development lifecycle. They ensure that features meet acceptance criteria, are free of regressions, and are safe to release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and execute test plans, test cases, and exploratory testing
- Own the Definition of Done as it relates to quality gates
- Identify, document, and triage defects
- Maintain and extend automated test suites
- Sign off on release readiness from a quality perspective

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Reduce manual testing effort through automation
- Ensure acceptance criteria are testable and validated before release

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies with the delivery team
- Bug reports and test summaries in the issue tracker
- Release sign-off notes shared with PM and Product Manager
- Collaboration with Developers on Definition of Done

**Collaboration notes:** Partners closely with Developers on acceptance criteria and test coverage. Provides release readiness feedback to Project Managers. Surfaces quality risks to Product Managers for prioritization decisions.

---

## Engineering Manager / Delivery Lead

### Role Summary
Engineering Managers and Delivery Leads are accountable for the health, capacity, and output of the engineering team. They balance people leadership with delivery oversight.

### Responsibilities
- Manage team capacity, staffing, and skill development
- Remove systemic blockers escalated by the delivery team
- Ensure engineering practices and standards are maintained
- Partner with Project Managers on timeline and resource planning
- Represent engineering in cross-functional leadership discussions

### Goals
- Sustain a healthy, high-performing team
- Ensure the team can deliver sustainably without burnout
- Align engineering efforts with business and product priorities

### Typical Communication
- Regular 1:1s with team members
- Leadership syncs with Product Managers and Project Managers
- Escalation path for unresolved delivery blockers
- Capacity planning updates during project initiation and planning phases

**Collaboration notes:** Works with Project Managers on resourcing and timeline commitments. Supports Developers in clearing systemic blockers. Coordinates with Product Managers on scope and trade-off decisions.

---

## Designer / UX Lead

### Role Summary
Designers and UX Leads are responsible for user experience quality. They translate user needs and product requirements into usable, accessible, and well-tested designs.

### Responsibilities
- Lead user research, discovery, and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Collaborate with Developers on design implementation and consistency
- Maintain and evolve the design system
- Ensure accessibility requirements are met

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce rework by resolving UX ambiguity early in the process
- Ensure design intent is preserved through development

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions with Developers and Product Managers
- Handoff documentation and annotated design specs
- Usability testing reports and insights shared with the wider team
- Participation in sprint planning and backlog refinement

**Collaboration notes:** Provides design specifications to Developers to reduce ambiguity. Collaborates with Product Managers on user research and feature scope. Flags UX risks to Project Managers during execution.

---

## Stakeholder / Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors provide strategic direction, business context, and decision-making authority. Sponsors are typically accountable for the business outcome and project funding.

### Responsibilities
- Define and communicate business goals and success criteria
- Provide timely decisions and approvals at key project milestones
- Escalate or resolve organizational blockers outside the team's control
- Review and accept major deliverables and releases

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver expected business value
- Minimize risk to the organization and customers
- Maintain visibility into progress without micromanaging delivery

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates from Project and Product Managers
- Milestone reviews and go/no-go decisions at release gates
- Escalation-triggered briefings for business-impacting issues
- Final sign-off on major releases and scope changes

**Collaboration notes:** Receives regular status updates from Project Managers. Provides strategic input to Product Managers on priorities. Serves as the final escalation point for decisions that exceed the team's authority.

---

## Support / Operations Representative

### Role Summary
Support and Operations Representatives ensure that released software is supportable, observable, and operationally stable. They provide a critical feedback loop between production and the delivery team.

### Responsibilities
- Monitor production health, incidents, and customer-reported issues
- Provide operational readiness input before releases
- Triage and route production issues to appropriate teams
- Document runbooks, escalation procedures, and known issues
- Represent operational risk in planning and release discussions

### Goals
- Minimize customer-facing impact from defects and incidents
- Ensure production systems remain observable and recoverable
- Reduce incident response time through clear runbooks and escalation paths

### Typical Communication
- Incident reports and post-mortems shared with PM and engineering
- Operational readiness sign-off before major releases
- Feedback on bug trends and support volume to Product Managers
- Participation in release planning and retrospectives

**Collaboration notes:** Partners with QA / Test Engineers on release readiness and known issues. Provides operational feedback to Product Managers for backlog prioritization. Works with Project Managers to schedule maintenance windows and operational activities.
